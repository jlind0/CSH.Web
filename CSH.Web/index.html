Securing Civilian Cyberspace Beyond DoD Specifications

In physical space a body provides direct assertion for identification. In cyberspace a unique digital body is required to provide direct assertion as well, an IsoNuclei. Just like a body arrives at a physical bank, an IsoNuclei arrives at the digital bank. Both a body and an IsoNuclei exist independent of secondary identification credentials making them a unique factor.

Cyber security is at a crossroads. In a binary environment one fork is continuing indirect assertion of identity. It has a historical record of inadequacy. The other fork is direct assertion of identity with a digital body, an IsoNuclei.

An IsoNuclei based security model creates a unique secure execution environment as a base of operation. Within that environment secure activity is performed, a virtual digital SCIF.

Think of the possibilities with an IsoNuclei. An IsoNuclei can be used at any time from any device without leaving a footprint. An access control model basing usage on the presence of a digital body will substantially enhance the performance of other deployed cyber security solutions.

An IsoNuclei provides a unique second factor at the endpoint AND the point-of-authentication, true two-unique-factor-based secure activity.

Cyberspace with an IsoNuclei is the next frontier!
